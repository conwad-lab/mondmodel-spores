# External signal to verified positioning memo

## Summary

An external thought-leadership article or a competitor's public material can
be turned into a durable, board-usable positioning artifact — but only if the
mapping is done HONESTLY, against the platform's ACTUAL verified mechanisms,
never its aspirational ones. Done right, the memo-writing process is not just
a deliverable: it is a second-order verification pass over your own canon and
over your own knowledge-cutoff blind spots.

Two by-products proved more valuable than the positioning prose itself: the
process caught two pre-existing factual errors in an already-merged reference
file (a garbled analogy and a term mis-attributed to an article's authors),
and it forced a fresh live-verification of one regulatory date-claim that lay
beyond the model's training cutoff — confirming it rather than assuming it.

## The genome (replicable procedure)

1. **Read the source first-hand.** An operator-provided full copy beats a
   paywall reconstructed from secondary coverage. When reconstruction is
   unavoidable, label the access/confidence level explicitly — never launder
   a secondary summary as if it were the primary text.
2. **Separate article-attributed claims from independently-verified ones.**
   Every research finding gets labelled "as reported by <source>"; do not
   assert the underlying studies as settled unless separately checked.
   Cap verbatim quotation tightly and attribute every quote.
3. **Verify the flattering fact and the post-cutoff fact hardest.** A claim
   that makes your own product look good, or a claim about an event after
   your knowledge cutoff, is exactly where confident-but-wrong hides. Run
   a live search; do not accept it because it is plausible or convenient.
4. **Treat the memo as an audit of your own canon.** Mapping the external
   material to your real mechanisms will surface drift in those mechanisms'
   own reference files — fix it as a separate, hash-safe change if the
   underlying artifact is version-pinned.
5. **Map article-concept to ACTUAL mechanism, never a described capability
   that isn't built.** Name the real gate, the real audit chain, the real
   flow — not the roadmap item that sounds like it.
6. **Write an explicit "honest limits / open gaps" section as a hard
   requirement.** A mapping with zero open gaps is a red flag, not a clean
   pass — scrutinize it harder, not less.
7. **Adversarially verify the finished memo** against a live re-fetch of the
   source (fidelity), the verified state of your own system (no capability
   overreach), and copyright limits, before treating it as final.
8. **File as a dated, cross-linked doc.** A single memo is a reaction; a
   series that references its siblings compounds into a thesis.

## Gates that pass falsely (why unverified positioning survives review)

- A memo can read as authoritative and well-cited while every citation is to
  the *source's own claims*, with zero independent checking — sounding
  rigorous is not the same as being verified.
- Capability language borrowed from the external material ("auditable",
  "governed", "evidence-backed") can bleed into describing your own platform
  in the same register even where your own version is aspirational, not
  built — the prose reads consistent because the vocabulary is consistent,
  not because the claims are equally true.
- A recent-events claim that "sounds right" given known context is exactly
  the shape of a plausible fabrication a knowledge-cutoff model would
  produce — plausibility is not evidence, especially post-cutoff.

## Counterfactuals (what happens without this genome)

- The memo ships with an inherited factual error from an already-merged
  canon file, because nobody re-reads the canon while writing new prose
  about it — the two are treated as unrelated tasks.
- A confidently-stated but unverified post-cutoff date or event ends up in a
  client-facing artifact, discoverable by the first knowledgeable reader and
  costing more credibility than the memo built.
- Competitor claims get accepted or refuted based on their own marketing
  copy rather than an independent check of their public surface, producing
  a competitive analysis that is really just their press release restated.
- Positioning work accumulates as disconnected one-offs instead of a
  cross-linked series, so no compounding thesis ever forms.

## Transfer surface

Any LLM-assisted competitive analysis, thought-leadership response, board
memo, or "how we compare to X" artifact — especially where the source
material is paywalled, where competitor capability claims are unverifiable
beyond their public surface, or where a load-bearing fact may postdate the
model's training data.
